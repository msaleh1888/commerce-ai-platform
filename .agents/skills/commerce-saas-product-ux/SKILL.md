# Commerce SaaS Product UX

Use this skill as the product and UX decision lens for the commerce AI SaaS platform.

## Core Product Lens

Optimize for a premium B2B SaaS operations product used by catalog managers, merchandisers, retailer administrators, and AI/data engineers.

Favor:

- Clear operational workflows.
- Dense but readable information design.
- Trustworthy evidence and provenance.
- Human approval for risky catalog changes.
- Measured AI quality instead of vague AI claims.
- Tenant safety and permission clarity.
- Professional polish that supports real work.

Avoid:

- Generic AI dashboards.
- Decorative UI that does not improve workflow comprehension.
- Vague metrics or unsupported claims.
- Autonomous mutation without review.
- Oversized marketing-style layouts inside the app.
- Features that distract from the MVP demo path.

## Workflow

1. Identify the primary persona and their goal.
2. Identify where the decision fits in the demo journey or product workflow.
3. Evaluate the decision against clarity, trust, speed, evidence, and safety.
4. Decide whether the feature or screen belongs in MVP, next release, or later.
5. Recommend concrete product, UX, and implementation changes.
6. Call out missing states: empty, loading, error, permission denied, partial success, and audit/history.

## Output Expectations

When giving recommendations:

- Start with the highest-impact product or UX decision.
- Tie advice to the relevant persona or portfolio reviewer.
- Prefer specific screen/workflow changes over abstract design advice.
- Separate MVP requirements from later enhancements.
- Keep public portfolio credibility in mind: claims must be visible, testable, or measurable.
